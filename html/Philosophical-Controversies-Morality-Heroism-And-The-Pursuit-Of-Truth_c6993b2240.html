  • Students debated Aristotle's claim that happiness is the point of life by examining the differences between fleeting happiness and lasting fulfillment.   • Students integrated biological imperatives and ethical callings to challenge the idea of a universal point of life.   • Guide played a key role in deepening the discussion by asking probing questions about Aristotles concept of eudaimonia and the distinction between lifes purpose and its meaning. It pushed the students to clarify their ideas on how personal fulfillment, morality, and biological imperatives interact in forming ones point of life.

Student 1, how would you respond to Student 2's challenging question? If someone derives happiness from harmful actions, does that still fulfill Aristotle's vision of happiness as the point of life?
